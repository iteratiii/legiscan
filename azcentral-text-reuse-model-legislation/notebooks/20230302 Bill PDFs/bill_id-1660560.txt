HOUSE BILL No. 1346
_____

DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL

Citations Affected:  IC 20-33-14.

Synopsis:  Gender fluidity. Provides that a school may not promote or
encourage the use of, or require, compel, or coerce a student, an
employee of the school, or a staff member of the school to use: (1) a
pronoun, title, or other word to identify a student, school employee, or
other individual that is inconsistent with the student's, employee's, or
individual's biological sex; or (2) a name or nickname to identify a
student that is inconsistent with the student's name on the student's
birth certificate. Creates an exception. Provides that a school may not
require an employee or a staff member of the school to adopt, support,
or promote gender fluidity.

Effective:  July 1, 2023.

Teshka, King, Carbaugh, Wesco

January 17, 2023, read first time and referred to Committee on Education.

4 Chapter 14. Gender Identification
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "gender fluidity" means any
6 theory or ideology that adopts any of the following views:
7 (1) Sex is a social construct.
8 (2) It is possible for an individual to be:
9 (A) any gender; or

10 (B) no gender;
11 based on the individual's feelings or preferences.
12 (3) An individual's sex can be changed to align with the
13 gender that:
14 (A) the individual identifies with or expresses; and
15 (B) is inconsistent with the individual's sex.
16 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "health care provider" means
17 any of the following:
