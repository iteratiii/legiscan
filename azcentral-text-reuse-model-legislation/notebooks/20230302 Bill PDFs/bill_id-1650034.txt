DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL

Citations Affected:  IC 12-15-1-23; IC 16-18-2; IC 16-35-12;
IC 27-2-28; IC 34-18-1-1.

Synopsis:  Gender transition procedures for minors. Prohibits specified
health care professionals from providing gender transition procedures
to a minor that are intended to alter the gender of the minor or delay
puberty. Provides for certain medical exceptions. Prohibits public
funds, Medicaid, or a health carrier from providing reimbursement or
coverage for gender transition procedures to a minor. Creates a civil
cause of action. Makes conforming changes.

Effective:  July 1, 2023.

Lauer

January 10, 2023, read first time and referred to Committee on Public Health.

13 Chapter 12. Gender Transition Procedures for Minors
14 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "biological sex" means the
15 biological indication of male and female in the context of
16 reproductive potential or capacity, including sex chromosomes,
17 naturally occurring sex hormones, gonads, and nonambiguous
18 internal and external genitalia present at birth, without regard to
19 an individual's psychological, chosen, or subjective experience of
20 gender.
21 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "cross sex hormones" means the
22 following:
23 (1) Testosterone or other androgens given to biological sex
24 females in amounts that are larger or more potent than would
25 normally occur naturally in healthy biological sex females.
26 (2) Estrogen given to biological sex males in amounts that are
27 larger or more potent than would normally occur naturally in
28 healthy biological sex males.
29 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "gender" means the
30 psychological, behavioral, social, and cultural aspects of being male
31 or female.
32 Sec. 4. As used in this chapter, "gender reassignment surgery"
33 means any medical or surgical service that seeks to surgically alter
34 or remove healthy physical or anatomical characteristics or
35 features that are typical for the individual's biological sex, in order
36 to instill or create physiological or anatomical characteristics that
37 resemble a sex different from the individual's biological sex,
38 including genital gender reassignment surgery or nongenital
39 gender reassignment surgery performed for the purpose of
40 assisting an individual with a gender transition.
41 Sec. 5. As used in this chapter, "gender transition" means the
42 process in which an individual shifts from identifying with and
