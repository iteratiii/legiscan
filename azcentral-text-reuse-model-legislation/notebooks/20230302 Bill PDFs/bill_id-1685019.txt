A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING MINORS FROM ATTENDING DRAG SHOWS; 13 

PROHIBITING MINORS FROM ENTERING SEXUALLY ORIENTED BUSINESSES; PROHIBITING DRAG 14 

PERFORMANCES IN LIBRARIES OR SCHOOLS THAT RECEIVE STATE FUNDING; PROVIDING 15 

DEFINITIONS; AND PROVIDING PENALTIES.” 16 

 17 

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA: 18 

 19 

NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 2], the following definitions 20 

apply: 21 

(1) "Drag performance" means a performance in which a performer exhibits a gender identity that 22 

is different than the performer's gender assigned at birth using clothing, makeup, or other physical markers and 23 

sings, lip syncs, dances, or otherwise performs for entertainment to appeal to a prurient interest. 24 

(2) "Nude" means: 25 

(a) entirely unclothed; or 26 

(b) clothed in a manner that leaves uncovered or visible through less than fully opaque clothing 27 

any portion of the breast below the top of the areola of the breasts if the person is female or any portion of the 28

(3) "Sexually oriented business" means a nightclub, bar, restaurant, or similar commercial 2

(a) provides for an audience of two or more individuals: 4 

(i) live nude entertainment or live nude performances; or 5 

(ii) a drag performance; and 6 

(b) authorizes on-premises consumption of alcoholic beverages. 7 

 8 

NEW SECTION. Section 2. Restrictions on sexually oriented businesses -- penalty. (1) A 9 

sexually oriented business may not allow a person under 18 years of age to enter the premises of the business. 10 

(2) The owner, operator, manager, or employee of a sexually oriented business who is convicted 11 

of violating this section shall be fined not less than $1,000 or more than $5,000 for the first offense, not less 12 

than $2,500 or more than $5,000 for the second offense, and for third and subsequent offenses be fined 13 

$10,000 and, if applicable, the county or municipality shall revoke the business license held by the offender. 14 

 15 

NEW SECTION. Section 3. Drag performances prohibited in publicly funded libraries or 16 

schools. (1) A library that receives any form of funding from the state may not allow a drag performance as 17 

defined in [section 1] on its premises during its regular operating hours. 18 

(2) A school that receives any form of funding from the state may not allow a drag performance as 19 

defined in [section 1] on its premises during school hours or at any school-sanctioned extracurricular activity. 20 

(3) A library, a school, or library or school personnel convicted of violating the prohibition under this 21 

section shall be fined $5,000 and, if applicable, the board of public education shall initiate proceedings to 22 

suspend the teacher, administrator, or specialist certificate of the offender under 20-4-110 for 1 year. If an 23 

offender's certificate has previously been suspended pursuant to this subsection (3), the board of public 24 

education shall initiate proceedings to permanently revoke the teacher, administrator, or specialist certificate of 25 

the offender under 20-4-110 on a subsequent violation of this section. 26
