A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE REQUIREMENTS FOR HUMAN SEXUALITY 4 

INSTRUCTION IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS; PROHIBITING SCHOOLS FROM PROVIDING HUMAN SEXUALITY 5 

INSTRUCTION TO STUDENTS IN GRADE 5 OR BELOW; PROHIBITING SCHOOLS FROM PROVIDING 6 

INSTRUCTION TO ANY STUDENT ABOUT GENDER IDENTITY, GENDER EXPRESSION, SEXUAL 7 

ORIENTATION, OR SEXUAL RELATIONSHIP; REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "HUMAN SEXUALITY 8 

INSTRUCTION"; AMENDING SECTION 20-7-120, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.” 9 

 10 

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA: 11 

 12 

Section 1. Section 20-7-120, MCA, is amended to read: 13 

"20-7-120.  Excused Human sexuality instruction -- excused absences from curriculum 14 

requirements -- notice -- prohibited activities. (1) (a) In recognition of parents' rights to direct the education 15 

of their own children, a school district may not provide human sexuality instruction to any child who is enrolled 16 

in grade 5 or below. 17 

(b) A parent, guardian, or other person who is responsible for the care of a child enrolled in grades 18 

6 through 12 may refuse to allow the child to attend or may withdraw the child from a course of instruction, a 19 

class period, an assembly, an organized school function, or instruction provided by the district through its staff 20 

or guests invited at the request of the district regarding human sexuality instruction. The withdrawal or refusal to 21 

attend is an excused absence pursuant to 20-5-103. 22 

(2) Any school implementing or maintaining a curriculum, providing materials, or holding an event 23 

or assembly at which the district provides human sexuality instruction, whether introduced by school educators, 24 

administrators, or officials or by guests invited at the request of the school, shall adopt a policy ensuring 25 

parental or guardian notification no less than 48 hours prior to holding an event or assembly or introducing 26 

materials for instructional use. 27 

(3) A school district shall annually notify the parent or guardian of each student scheduled to be 28
