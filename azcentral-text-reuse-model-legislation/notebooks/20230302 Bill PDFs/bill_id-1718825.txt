AN ACT relating to birth certificates. 1 

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky: 2 

Section 1.   KRS 213.046 is amended to read as follows: 3 

(1) A certificate of birth for each live birth which occurs in the Commonwealth shall be 4 

filed with the state registrar within five (5) working days after such birth and shall 5 

be registered if it has been completed and filed in accordance with this section and 6 

applicable administrative regulations. No certificate shall be held to be complete 7 

and correct that does not supply all items of information called for in this section 8 

and in KRS 213.051, or satisfactorily account for their omission except as provided 9 

in KRS 199.570(3). If a certificate of birth is incomplete, the local registrar shall 10 

immediately notify the responsible person and require that person to supply the 11 

missing items, if that information can be obtained. 12 

(2) When a birth occurs in an institution or en route thereto, the person in charge of the 13 

institution or that person's designated representative, shall obtain the personal data, 14 

prepare the certificate, secure the signatures required, and file the certificate as 15 

directed in subsection (1) of this section or as otherwise directed by the state 16 

registrar within the required five (5) working days. The physician or other person in 17 

attendance shall provide the medical information required for the certificate and 18 

certify to the fact of birth within five (5) working days after the birth. If the 19 

physician or other person in attendance does not certify to the fact of birth within 20 

the five (5) working day period, the person in charge of the institution shall 21 

complete and sign the certificate. 22 

(3) When a birth occurs in a hospital or en route thereto to a woman who is unmarried, 23 

the person in charge of the hospital or that person's designated representative shall 24 

immediately before or after the birth of a child, except when the mother or the 25 

alleged father is a minor: 26 

(a) Meet with the mother prior to the release from the hospital; 27

(9) The following provisions shall apply if the mother was married at the time of either 5 

conception or birth or anytime between conception and birth: 6 

(a) If there is no dispute as to paternity, the name of the husband shall be entered 7 

on the certificate as the father of the child. The surname of the child shall be 8 

any name chosen by the parents; however, if the parents are separated or 9 

divorced at the time of the child's birth, the choice of surname rests with the 10 

parent who has legal custody following birth;[.] 11 

(b) If the mother claims that the father of the child is not her husband and the 12 

husband agrees to such a claim and the putative father agrees to the statement, 13 

a three (3) way affidavit of paternity may be signed by the respective parties 14 

and duly notarized. The state registrar of vital statistics shall enter the name of 15 

a nonhusband on the birth certificate as the father and the surname of the child 16 

shall be any name chosen by the mother; and[.] 17 

(c) If a question of paternity determination arises which is not resolved under 18 

paragraph (b) of this subsection, it shall be settled by the District Court. 19 

(10) The following provisions shall apply if the mother was not married at the time of 20

Section 2.   KRS 213.056 is amended to read as follows: 14 

(1) If a certificate of birth of a living person born in the Commonwealth has not been 15 

filed within the time period as provided in KRS 213.046, a certificate of birth may 16 

be filed in accordance with the administrative regulations of the cabinet. The 17 

certificate shall be registered subject to such evidentiary requirements as the cabinet 18 

shall by regulation prescribe to substantiate the alleged facts of birth. 19 

(2) In accordance with the provisions of this section and the administrative regulations 20 

established thereunder, the state registrar may issue a record of foreign birth for a 21 

person born outside the United States registration area who is subsequently adopted 22 

by a Kentucky resident and whose record of birth cannot be obtained from the 23 

country of birth. 24 

(3) Certificates of birth registered one (1) year or more after the date of birth shall be 25 

made on forms prescribed and furnished by the state registrar marked "delayed" and 26 

shall show on the face of the certificate the date of the delayed registration. 27
