SECTION 1.   A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 344 IS CREATED TO 4

As used in Sections 1 to 4 of this Act: 6 

(1) "Conscience" means the religious, moral or ethical principles held by a medical 7 

practitioner, a health care institution, or a health care payer. For purposes of 8 

Sections 1 to 4 of this act, a health care institution or health care payer's 9 

conscience shall be determined by reference to its existing or proposed religious, 10 

moral or ethical guidelines, mission statement, constitution, bylaws, articles of 11 

incorporation, regulations or other relevant documents; 12 

(2) "Disclosure" means a formal or informal communication or transmission, but 13 

does not include a communication or transmission concerning policy decisions 14 

that lawfully exercise discretionary authority, unless the medical practitioner 15 

providing the disclosure or transmission reasonably believes that the disclosure 16 

or transmission evinces: 17 

(a) A violation of any law, rule, or regulation; 18 

(b) A violation of any standard of care or other ethical guidelines for the 19 

provision of any health care service; or 20 

(c) Gross mismanagement, a gross waste of funds, an abuse of authority, or a 21 

substantial and specific danger to public health or safety; 22 

(3) "Discrimination" means any adverse action taken against, or any threat of 23 

adverse action communicated to, a medical practitioner, health care institution, 24 

or health care payer as a result of his, her, or its decision to decline to participate 25 

in a health care service on the basis of conscience. Discrimination includes but is 26 

not limited to termination of employment; transfer from current position; 27

SECTION 2.   A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 344 IS CREATED TO 22 

READ AS FOLLOWS: 23 

(1) A medical practitioner, health care institution, or health care payer shall have the 24 

right not to participate in or pay for any health care service which violates his, 25 

her, or its conscience. 26 

(2) A medical practitioner, health care institution, or health care payer shall not be 27

(3) No medical practitioner, health care institution, or health care payer shall be 3

SECTION 3.   A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 344 IS CREATED TO 10 

READ AS FOLLOWS: 11 

(1) No medical practitioner shall be discriminated against because he or she: 12 

(a) Provided, caused to be provided, or is about to provide to his or her 13 

employer, the Attorney General, any agency of the Commonwealth charged 14 

with protecting health care rights of conscience, the United States 15 

Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights, or any 16 

other federal agency charged with protecting health care rights of 17 

conscience with information relating to any violation, act, or omission the 18 

medical practitioner reasonably believes to be a violation of any provision of 19 

Sections 1 to 4 of this Act; 20 

(b) Testified or is about to testify in a proceeding concerning a violation; or 21 

(c) Assisted, participated, or is about to assist or participate in related 22 

proceedings. 23 

(2) Unless the disclosure is prohibited by law, no medical practitioner shall be 24 

discriminated against because he or she disclosed information that he or she 25 

reasonably believe evinces: 26 

(a) A violation of a relevant law, rule, or regulation; 27

SECTION 4.   A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 344 IS CREATED TO 5 

READ AS FOLLOWS: 6 

Any person injured by a violation of Sections 1 to 4 of this Act shall have a civil cause 7 

of action in the Circuit Court of appropriate jurisdiction to enjoin further violations, to 8 

recover the actual damages sustained, and to recover the costs of the lawsuit and to 9 

provide any other appropriate relief, which may include reinstatement of a medical 10 

practitioner to his or her previous position, reinstatement of board certification, and 11 

relicensure of a health care institution or health care payer. If a violation is found to 12 

have occurred, the court's order or judgment shall include a reasonable fee for the 13 

plaintiff's attorney of record. Any additional burden or expense on another medical 14 

practitioner, health care institution, or health care payer arising from the exercise of 15 

the right of conscience shall not be a defense to any violation of Sections 1 to 4 of this 16 

Act. 17 

Section 5.   Whereas it is crucial that the Commonwealth's regulatory policies 18 

reflect the statutory intent of the General assembly, an emergency is declared to exist, and 19 

this Act takes effect upon its passage and approval by the Governor or upon its otherwise 20 

becoming a law. 21
