A BILL for an Act to create and enact chapter 23-52 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating 

to prohibiting medical gender transitioning procedures on a minor; to provide a penalty; and to 

declare an emergency.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:

SECTION 1. Chapter 23-52 of the North Dakota Century Code is created and enacted as 

follows:

23  -  52  -  01. Definitions.  

As used in this chapter:

1. "Congenital defect" means a physical or chemical abnormality present in a minor 

which is inconsistent with the normal development of an individual of the minor's sex, 

including abnormalities caused by a medically verifiable disorder of sex development. 

The term does not include gender dysphoria, gender identity disorder, gender 

incongruence, or any mental condition, disorder, disability, or abnormality.

2. "Health care provider" means a licensed physician, physician assistant, nurse, or a 

certified medical assistant.

3. "Medical procedure" includes surgically removing, modifying, altering, or entering 

tissues, cavities, or organs of an individual.

4. "Medication" means any drug prescribed, administered, or supplied to an individual 

which has the purpose of aligning the individual's sex with the individual's gender 

identity when the gender identity is inconsistent with the individual's sex. The term 

includes:

a. Puberty-blocking medication to stop normal puberty;

b. Supraphysiologic doses of testosterone to females; or

c. Supraphysiologic doses of estrogen to males.

5. "Minor" means an individual under the age of eighteen. The term includes an 

emancipated individual.

6. "Parent" means a biological, legal, or adoptive parent of a minor or a legal guardian of 

a minor.

7. "Sex" means the biological state of being female or male, based on an individual's 

nonambiguous sex organs, chromosomes, and endogenous hormone profiles at birth.

23  -  52  -  02. Gender transitioning procedure on a minor prohibited   -   Exceptions.  

1. A health care provider may not perform or offer to perform a medical procedure on a 

minor, or administer or offer to administer medication to a minor, if the performance of 

the medical procedure or administration of the medication is for the purpose of:

a. Enabling a minor to identify with, or live as, a purported identity inconsistent with 

the minor's sex; or

b. Treating purported discomfort or distress from a discordance between the minor's 

sex and asserted identity.

2. It is not a violation of subsection     1 if a health care provider performs or offers to   

perform a medical procedure on or administers, or offers to administer medication to a 

minor if:

a. The performance of the medical procedure or the administration of the 

medication is to treat a minor's congenital defect, disease, or physical injury; or

b. The performance of the medical procedure or medication protocol began before 

the effective date of this Act.

3. For purposes of subdivision     b of subsection     2, the minor's treating physician shall   

certify in writing that, in the physician's good-faith medical judgment, based on the 

facts known to the physician at the time, ending the medical procedure or medication 

would be harmful to the minor. The certification must include the findings supporting 

the certification and must be made a part of the minor's medical record.

4. Subdivision     b of subsection     2 does not allow a health care provider to perform a   

medical procedure or administer medication that is different from the medical 

procedure performed or medication administered before the effective date of this Act 

when the primary purpose of the different medical procedure or medication is to:

5. Subdivision     b of subsection     2, does not abrogate a minor's right to bring a cause of

6. It is not a defense to legal liability incurred as the result of a violation of this section

8. As used in this section, "disease" does not include gender dysphoria, gender identity

abnormality.

23  -  52  -  03. Private right of action.  

1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection     3, a minor, or the parent of a minor, injured   

as a result of a violation of section 23  -  52  -  02, may bring a civil cause of action to   

recover compensatory damages, punitive damages, and reasonable attorney's fees, 

court costs, and expenses.

2. A minor injured as a result of a violation of section 23  -  52  -  02 may bring a civil cause of   

action against the:

a. Health care provider alleged to have violated section 23  -  52  -  02;  

b. Medical facility employing the health care provider; and

c. Minor's parent if the parent of the minor consented to the conduct that constituted 

the violation on behalf of the minor. This subdivision supersedes any common 

law immunity granted to a parent.

3. The parent of a minor injured as a result of a violation of section 23  -  52  -  02 may bring a   

civil cause of action against a health care provider or the medical facility employing the 

health care provider alleged to have violated section 23  -  52  -  02. The parent of a minor   

injured as a result of a violation of section 23  -  52  -  02 may not bring a civil cause of   

action against a health care provider or the medical facility employing the health care 

provider if the parent consented to the conduct that constituted the violation on behalf 

of the minor.

4. The parent or next of kin of a minor may bring a wrongful death action, under chapter 

32  -  21, against a health care provider or the medical facility employing the health care   

provider alleged to have violated section 23  -  52  -  02, if the injured minor is deceased   

and:

a. The minor's death is the result of the physical or emotional harm inflicted upon 

the minor by the violation; and

b. The parent of the minor did not consent to the conduct that constituted the 

violation on behalf of the minor.

5. If a court in a civil action brought under this section finds a health care provider or the 

administration of a medical facility employing the health care provider knowingly 

violated section 23  -  52  -  02, the court shall notify the appropriate regulatory authority,   

the appropriate state's attorney, and the attorney general by mailing a certified copy of 

the court's order to the regulatory authority, the appropriate state's attorney, and the 

attorney general. The notification must be mailed when the final judgment is entered.

6. Compensatory damages under this section may include:

a. Reasonable economic losses caused by the emotional, mental, or physical 

effects of the violation, including:

(1) The cost of counseling, hospitalization, and any other medical expenses 

associated with treating the harm caused by the violation;

(2) Any out  -  of-pocket costs of the minor paid to the health care provider for the   

prohibited medical procedure or medication; and

(3) Loss of income caused by the violation; and

b. Noneconomic damages caused by the violation, including psychological and 

emotional anguish.

7. Notwithstanding chapter 28  -  01, an action commenced under this section must be   

brought:

a. Within thirty years from the date the minor reaches eighteen years of age; or

b. Within ten years of the minor's death if the minor is deceased.

23  -  52  -  04. Public right of action.  

1. The attorney general shall establish a process by which violations of section 23  -  52  -  02   

may be reported.

2. Within twenty years of the violation, the attorney general or appropriate state's 

attorney may investigate any alleged violation of section 23  -  52  -  02. If there is probable   

cause to believe that a violation has occurred, the attorney general or appropriate 

state's attorney may bring an action against a health care provider that knowingly 

violates section 23  -  52  -  02 to enjoin further violations, to disgorge any profits received   

due to the medical procedure or medication, and to recover a civil penalty of:

a. Twenty  -  five thousand dollars per violation if the violation involved the surgical   

removal, modification, alteration, or entering of tissues, cavities, or organs of an 

individual; and

b. Twenty thousand dollars per violation if the violation involved prescribing, 

administering, dispensing, or otherwise supplying any drug or device to an 

individual.

3. A civil penalty collected under this section must be paid into the state general fund.

4. The attorney general or appropriate state's attorney is entitled to reasonable attorney's 

fees, court costs, and expenses if the attorney general or state's attorney prevails in 

an action brought under this section.

5. Jurisdiction for an action brought under this section is the district court in the judicial 

district serving Burleigh County or in the judicial district where the violation occurred.

23  -  52  -  05. Health care provider licensing sanctions.  

A violation of section 23  -  52  -  02 constitutes a potential threat to public health, safety, and   

welfare and requires emergency action by an alleged violator's appropriate regulatory authority. 

Upon receiving notification pursuant to subsection     5 of section 23  -  52  -  03, or upon otherwise   

becoming aware of an alleged violation of section 23  -  52  -  02, the appropriate regulatory authority   

shall proceed with appropriate disciplinary action.

23  -  52  -  06. Minor immunity.  

A minor upon whom a medical procedure is performed or to whom medication is 

administered may not be held liable for violating section 23  -  52  -  02.  

SECTION 2. EMERGENCY. This Act is declared to be an emergency measure.
