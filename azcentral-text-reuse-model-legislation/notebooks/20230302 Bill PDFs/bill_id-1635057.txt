AN ACT 

To amend chapter 191, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to gender transition 

procedures. 
 

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows: 

     Section A.  Chapter 191, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto 1 

one new section, to be known as section 191.1720, to read as 2 

follows:3 

     191.1720.  1.  This section shall be known and may be  1 

cited as the "Missouri Save Adolescents from Experimentation  2 

(SAFE) Act". 3 

     2.  For purposes of this section, the following terms  4 

mean: 5 

     (1)  "Biological sex", the biological indication of  6 

male or female in the context of reproductive potential or  7 

capacity, such as sex chromosomes, naturally occurring sex  8 

hormones, gonads, and nonambiguous internal and external  9 

genitalia present at birth, without regard to an  10 

individual's psychological, chosen, or subjective experience  11 

of gender; 12 

     (2)  "Cross-sex hormones": 13 

     (a)  Testosterone or other androgens given to  14 

biological sex females in amounts that are larger or more  15 

potent than would normally occur naturally in healthy  16 

biological sex females; and 17 

     (b)  Estrogen given to biological sex males in amounts  18 

that are larger or more potent than would normally occur  19 

naturally in healthy biological sex males; 20

     (3)  "Gender", the psychological, behavioral, social,  21 

and cultural aspects of being male or female; 22 

     (4)  "Gender reassignment surgery", any medical or  23 

surgical service that seeks to surgically alter or remove  24 

healthy physical or anatomical characteristics or features  25 

that are typical for the individual's biological sex in  26 

order to instill or create physiological or anatomical   27 

characteristics that resemble a sex different from the  28 

individual's biological sex, including, but not limited to,  29 

genital or nongenital gender reassignment surgery performed  30 

for the purpose of assisting an individual with a gender  31 

transition; 32 

     (5)  "Gender transition", the process in which an  33 

individual transitions from identifying with and living as a  34 

gender that corresponds to his or her biological sex to  35 

identifying and living as a gender different from his or her  36 

biological sex, and may involve social, legal, or physical  37 

changes; 38 

     (6)  "Gender transition procedures": 39 

     (a)  Any medical or surgical service, including, but  40 

not limited to, physician's services, inpatient and  41 

outpatient hospital services, or prescribed drugs, related  42 

to gender transition that seeks to: 43 

     a.  Alter or remove physical or anatomical  44 

characteristics or features that are typical for the  45 

individual's biological sex; or 46 

     b.  Instill or create physiological or anatomical  47 

characteristics that resemble a sex different from the  48 

individual's biological sex, including, but not limited to: 49 

     (i)  Medical services that provide puberty-blocking  50 

drugs, cross-sex hormones, or other mechanisms to promote  51

the development of feminizing or masculinizing features in  52 

the opposite biological sex; or 53 

     (ii)  Genital or nongenital gender reassignment surgery  54 

performed for the purpose of assisting an individual with a  55 

gender transition; 56 

     (b)  The term "gender transition procedures" shall not  57 

include: 58 

     a.  Services to individuals born with a medically- 59 

verifiable disorder of sex development, including, but not  60 

limited to, an individual with external biological sex  61 

characteristics that are irresolvably ambiguous, such as  62 

those born with forty-six XX chromosomes with virilization,  63 

forty-six XY chromosomes with undervirilization, or having  64 

both ovarian and testicular tissue; 65 

     b.  Services provided when a physician has otherwise  66 

diagnosed an individual with a disorder of sexual  67 

development and determined through genetic or biochemical  68 

testing that the individual does not have normal sex  69 

chromosome structure, sex steroid hormone production, or sex  70 

steroid hormone action; 71 

     c.  The treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or  72 

disorder that has been caused by or exacerbated by the  73 

performance of gender transition procedures regardless of  74 

whether the gender transition procedure was performed in  75 

accordance with state and federal law; or 76 

     d.  Any procedure undertaken because the individual  77 

suffers from a physical disorder, physical injury, or  78 

physical illness that would, as certified by a physician,  79 

place the individual in imminent danger of death or  80 

impairment of a major bodily function unless surgery is   81 

performed; 82

     (7)  "Genital gender reassignment surgery", a medical  83 

procedure performed for the purpose of assisting an  84 

individual with a gender transition, including, but not  85 

limited to: 86 

     (a)  Surgical procedures such as penectomy,  87 

orchiectomy, vaginoplasty, clitoroplasty, or vulvoplasty for  88 

biologically male patients or hysterectomy or ovariectomy  89 

for biologically female patients; 90 

     (b)  Reconstruction of the fixed part of the urethra  91 

with or without a metoidioplasty; or 92 

     (c)  Phalloplasty, vaginectomy, scrotoplasty, or  93 

implantation of erection or testicular prostheses for  94 

biologically female patients; 95 

     (8)  "Health care provider", an individual who is  96 

licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized by the laws of  97 

this state to administer health care in the ordinary course  98 

of the practice of his or her profession; 99 

     (9)  "Nongenital gender reassignment surgery", medical  100 

procedures performed for the purpose of assisting an  101 

individual with a gender transition, including, but not  102 

limited to: 103 

     (a)  Surgical procedures for biologically male  104 

patients, such as augmentation mammoplasty, facial  105 

feminization surgery, liposuction, lipofilling, voice  106 

surgery, thyroid cartilage reduction, gluteal augmentation,  107 

hair reconstruction, or various aesthetic procedures; or 108 

     (b)  Surgical procedures for biologically female  109 

patients, such as subcutaneous mastectomy, voice surgery,  110 

liposuction, lipofilling, pectoral implants, or various  111 

aesthetic procedures; 112 

     (10)  "Physician", an individual who is licensed under  113 

chapter 334; 114

     (11)  "Puberty-blocking drugs", gonadotropin-releasing  115 

hormone analogues or other synthetic drugs used in  116 

biological sex males to stop luteinizing hormone secretion  117 

and therefore testosterone secretion, or synthetic drugs  118 

used in biological sex females that stop the production of  119 

estrogens and progesterone, when used to delay or suppress  120 

pubertal development in children for the purpose of  121 

assisting an individual with a gender transition. 122 

     3.  A physician or other health care provider shall not  123 

provide gender transition procedures to any individual under  124 

eighteen years of age and shall not refer any individual  125 

under eighteen years of age to any health care provider for  126 

gender transition procedures. 127 

     4.  Any referral for or provision of gender transition  128 

procedures to an individual under eighteen years of age  129 

shall be considered unprofessional conduct and any health  130 

care provider doing so may be subject to discipline by the  131 

appropriate licensing entity or disciplinary review board  132 

with competent jurisdiction in this state. 133 

     5.  The provision of gender transition procedures to an  134 

individual under eighteen years of age in violation of the  135 

provisions of this section shall be considered grounds for a  136 

cause of action against the health care provider for  137 

personal injury or death under chapter 538 and such cause of  138 

action shall be governed by the provisions of such chapter.   139 

An individual under eighteen years of age upon whom gender  140 

transition procedures were performed in violation of this  141 

section may bring the cause of action either through a next  142 

friend or his or her own name, subject to the provisions of  143 

section 516.105. 144
