A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A YOUTH HEALTH PROTECTION ACT; 4 

PROHIBITING CERTAIN MEDICAL AND SURGICAL TREATMENTS TO TREAT MINORS WITH GENDER 5 

DYSPHORIA; PROHIBITING PUBLIC FUNDS, PROGRAMS, PROPERTY, AND EMPLOYEES FROM BEING 6 

USED FOR THESE TREATMENTS; PROVIDING THAT A HEALTH CARE PROFESSIONAL WHO VIOLATES 7 

THIS LAW COMMITS PROFESSIONAL MISCONDUCT; PROVIDING A PRIVATE CAUSE OF ACTION; 8 

PROHIBITING DISCHARGE OF PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY VIA INSURANCE; AND PROVIDING 9 

DEFINITIONS.” 10 

 11 

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA: 12 

 13 

NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 6] may be cited as the "Youth Health 14 

Protection Act". 15 

 16 

NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purpose of [sections 1 through 6] is to enhance the 17 

protection of minors, pursuant to Article II, section 15, of the Montana constitution, from any form of pressure to 18 

undergo irreversible medical procedures to change sex prior to attaining the age of majority. 19 

 20 

NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in this part, unless the context clearly indicates 21 

otherwise, the following definitions apply: 22 

(1) "Female" means an individual who is a member of the female sex. 23 

(2) "Gender" means the psychological, behavioral, social, and cultural aspects of being male or 24 

female. 25 

(3) "Gender dysphoria" is the diagnosis of gender dysphoria under the Diagnostic and Statistical 26 

Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition. 27 

(4) "Health care professional" means a person who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized 28

(5) "Male" means an individual who is a member of the male sex. 3 

(6) "Mental health professional" means a person who is licensed to diagnose and treat mental 4

(7) "Minor" means an individual under 18 years of age. 6 

(8) "Perceived gender" is a person's internal sense of his or her gender. 7

(12) "Social transitioning" means acts other than pharmaceutical or surgical interventions that are 14

sex, including the changing of a minor's preferred pronouns or dress and the recommendation to wear clothing 16 

or devices, such as binders, for the purpose of concealing a minor's secondary sex characteristics. 17

NEW SECTION. Section 4. Prohibitions. (1) Public funds may not be directly or indirectly used, 19 

granted, paid, or distributed to any individual, entity, or organization that provides or subsidizes medication or 20 

surgery as a treatment to address an inconsistency between a minor's sex and the minor's perceived gender or 21 

perceived sex. 22 

(2) Any individual or entity that receives state funds to pay or subsidize the treatment of minors for 23 

psychological conditions, including gender dysphoria, may not use state funds to promote or advocate 24 

medication or surgery as a treatment to address an inconsistency between a minor's sex and the minor's 25 

perceived gender or perceived sex. 26 

(3) Any amount paid by an individual or entity during a tax year for the provision of either 27 

medication or surgery as a treatment to address an inconsistency between a minor's sex and the minor's 28

NEW SECTION. Section 5. Private cause of action for subsequent harm. (1) Any health care 5 

professional or physician who provides puberty blockers, cross-sex hormones, or surgical procedures as a 6 

treatment to address an inconsistency between a minor's sex and the minor's perceived gender or perceived 7 

sex is strictly liable to that minor if the treatment or the after-effects of the treatment result in any injury, 8 

including physical, psychological, emotional, or physiological harms, within the next 25 years. 9 

(2) Except as provided in subsection (3), a person who suffers an injury described in subsection 10 

(1), or the person's legal guardian or estate, may bring a civil action either within 25 years from the day the 11 

person reaches 18 years of age or within 4 years from the time of discovery by the injured party of both the 12 

injury and the causal relationship between the treatment and the injury, whichever date is later, against the 13 

offending health care professional or physician in a court of competent jurisdiction for: 14 

(a) declaratory or injunctive relief; 15 

(b) compensatory damages, including but not limited to pain and suffering, loss of reputation, loss 16 

of income, and loss of consortium, including the loss of expectation of sharing parenthood; 17 

(c) punitive damages; 18 

(d) any other appropriate relief; and 19 

(e) attorney fees and costs. 20 

(3) (a) If, at the time the person subjected to treatment attains 18 years of age, the person is under 21 

other legal disability, the limitation period in subsection (2) does not begin to run until the removal of the 22 

disability. 23 

(b) The limitation period in subsection (2) does not run during a time period when the individual is 24 

subject to threats, intimidation, manipulation, fraudulent concealment, or fraud perpetrated by the health care 25 

professional or physician who provided the treatment described in subsection (1) or by any person acting in the 26 

interest of the health care professional or physician. 27 

(4) A health care professional or physician may not be indemnified for potential liability under this 28

NEW SECTION. Section 6. Prohibited insurance coverage. A professional liability insurance policy 7 

issued to a health care professional or physician may not include coverage for damages assessed against the 8

NEW SECTION. Section 7. Medical or surgical transition for minors. Failure of a health care 13 

professional, mental health professional, or physician to adhere to [section 4] constitutes unprofessional 14 

conduct, with a mandatory minimum suspension of the ability to practice the person's profession for 1 year. 15 

 16

Montana medicaid program may not reimburse or provide coverage for medication or surgery as a treatment to 18 

address an inconsistency between a minor's sex and the minor's perceived gender or perceived sex. 19
