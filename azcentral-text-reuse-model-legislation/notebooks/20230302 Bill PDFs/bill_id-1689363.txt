An Act to prevent government entities from entering contracts with companies that 1 

promote certain economic boycotts. 2 

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA: 3 

Section 1. That chapter 37-1 be amended with a NEW SECTION: 4 

Terms used in this Act mean: 5 

(1) "Company," a for-profit organization, association, corporation, partnership, joint 6 

venture, limited partnership, limited liability partnership, or limited liability 7 

company, and including a wholly owned subsidiary, majority-owned subsidiary, 8 

parent company, or affiliate of those entities or business associations. For purposes 9 

of this section, the term does not include sole proprietorships; 10 

(2) "Economic boycott," refusing to deal with, terminating business activities with, or 11 

otherwise taking any commercial action that is intended to penalize, inflict 12 

economic harm on, limit commercial relations with, or change or limit the activities 13 

of a company, for the primary purpose of achieving social, political, or ideological 14 

interests, because the company, without violating controlling federal or state law: 15 

(a) Engages in the exploration, production, utilization, transportation, sale, or 16 

manufacturing of, fossil fuel-based energy, timber, mining or agriculture;  17 

(b) Engages in, facilitates, or supports the manufacture, distribution, sale, or 18 

use of firearms;  19 

(c) Does not meet, is not expected to meet, or does not commit to meet 20 

corporate board, or employment, composition, compensation, or disclosure 21 

criteria that incorporates characteristics protected under chapter 20-13;  22 

(d) Does not facilitate, is not expected to facilitate, or does not commit to 23 

facilitate access to abortion, sex or gender change, or transgender surgery; 24 

or  25 

(e) Does business with a company described above. 26

(3) "Governmental entity," a state agency or political subdivision of this state; and 1 

(4) "Ordinary business purpose," does not include any purpose to further social, 2 

political, or ideological interests. A company may reasonably be determined to have 3 

taken an action, or considered a factor, with a purpose to further social, political, 4 

or ideological interests based upon evidence indicating such a purpose, including: 5 

(a) Branding, advertising, statements, explanations, reports, letters to clients, 6 

communications with portfolio companies, statements of principles, or 7 

commitments; or 8 

(b) Participation in, affiliation with, or status as a signatory to, any coalition, 9 

initiative, joint statement of principles, or agreement. 10 

Section 2. That chapter 37-1 be amended with a NEW SECTION: 11 

No state agency, elected or appointed official or employee of this state, or political 12 

subdivision of this state may, under any governmental authority, enter into a contract 13 

that is paid wholly or partly from public funds of the governmental entity, with any 14 

company for goods or services unless the contract contains a written verification from the 15 

company that it does not engage in economic boycotts and will not engage in economic 16 

boycotts during the term of the contract. 17 

Section 3. That chapter 37-1 be amended with a NEW SECTION: 18 

Section 2 of this Act does not apply to any governmental entity that determines 19 

the requirements are inconsistent with the governmental entity's constitutional or 20 

statutory duties related to the issuance, incurrence, or management of debt obligations 21 

or the deposit, custody, management, borrowing, or investment of funds; or the 22 

requirements prevent the governmental entity from obtaining the supplies or services to 23 

be provided in an economically practicable manner. 24 

Section 4. That chapter 37-1 be amended with a NEW SECTION: 25 

No party, whether the federal government, state government, or any of its 26 

agencies, departments, or political subdivisions, may take action to penalize or threaten 27 

to penalize any company or financial institution affiliated with said company for compliance 28 

with sections 1 to 4, inclusive, of this Act. 29
