_____

DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL

Citations Affected:  IC 16-18-2; IC 16-35-12.

Synopsis:  Prohibited health care for minors. Prohibits specified health
care professionals from: (1) performing certain medical procedures on
a minor; or (2) prescribing, administering, or furnishing certain drugs
to a minor; that purposely attempt to change, reinforce, or affirm a
minor's perception of the minor's own sexual attraction or sexual
behavior, or attempt to change, reinforce, or affirm a minor's gender
identity when the identity is inconsistent with the minor's biological
sex.

Effective:  July 1, 2023.

Borders

January 19, 2023, read first time and referred to Committee on Public Health.

1 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "health care professional" refers
2 to the following:
3 (1) A physician licensed under IC 25-22.5.
4 (2) A nurse, including an advanced practice registered nurse,

6 (3) Any of the behavioral health or human services
7 professionals licensed under IC 25-23.6.
8 (4) A physician assistant licensed under IC 25-27.5.
9 (5) A psychologist licensed under IC 25-33.

10 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "minor" means an individual
11 who is less than eighteen (18) years of age.
12 Sec. 3. Except as provided in section 5 of this chapter, a health
13 care professional may not purposely attempt to change, reinforce,
14 or affirm a minor's perception of the minor's own sexual attraction
15 or sexual behavior, or attempt to change, reinforce, or affirm a
16 minor's gender identity when the identity is inconsistent with the
17 minor's biological sex, by performing or causing to be performed
18 any of the following procedures on the minor:
19 (1) Castration.
20 (2) Vasectomy.
21 (3) Hysterectomy.
22 (4) Oophorectomy.
23 (5) Metoidioplasty.
24 (6) Orchiectomy.
25 (7) Penectomy.
26 (8) Phalloplasty.
27 (9) Urethroplasty.
28 (10) Vaginoplasty.
29 (11) Mastectomy.
30 (12) Lobotomy.
31 (13) A surgery to remove a healthy organ or body part.
32 Sec. 4. Except as provided in section 5 of this chapter, a health
33 care professional may not purposely attempt to change, reinforce,
34 or affirm a minor's perception of the minor's own sexual attraction
35 or sexual behavior, or attempt to change, reinforce, or affirm a
36 minor's gender identity when that identity is inconsistent with the
37 minor's biological sex, by prescribing, administering, or furnishing
38 to:
39 (1) the minor a drug to stop or delay puberty;
40 (2) a female minor testosterone or estrogen suppressing
41 drugs; or
42 (3) a male minor estrogen or testosterone suppressing drugs.
