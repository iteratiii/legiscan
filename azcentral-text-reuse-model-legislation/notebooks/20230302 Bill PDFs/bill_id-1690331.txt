SECTION 1.  Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article 6

"Article 1M. 8 

"Youth Health Protection Act. 9 

"§ 90-21.140.  Definitions. 10 

The following definitions apply in this Article: 11 

(1) Medical professional. – Any physician, surgeon, physician assistant, nurse, 12 

clinical nurse specialist, nurse practitioner, anesthetist, psychiatrist, or 13 

medical assistant licensed under this Chapter. 14 

(2) Minor. – Any individual who is below 18 years of age. 15 

(3) Political subdivision. – Any division of local government, county, city, 16 

assessment district, municipal corporation, special purpose district, board, 17 

department, commission, or any division of local government delegated the 18 

right to exercise part of the sovereign power of that subdivision. 19 

(4) Sex. – The biological state of being female or male, based on sex organs, 20 

chromosomes, and endogenous hormone profiles. 21 

"§ 90-21.141.  Prohibition of certain practices and health care services. 22 

(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, it shall be unlawful for any medical 23 

professional to engage in any of the following practices upon a minor, or cause them to be 24 

performed, to facilitate the minor's desire to present or appear in a manner that is inconsistent 25 

with the minor's sex: 26 

(1) Performing surgeries that sterilize, including castration, vasectomy, 27 

hysterectomy, oophorectomy, metoidioplasty, orchiectomy, penectomy, 28 

phalloplasty, and vaginoplasty. 29 

(2) Performing a mastectomy. 30 

(3) Administering or supplying the following medications that induce transient or 31 

permanent infertility: 32 

a. Puberty-blocking medication to stop or delay normal puberty. 33 

b. Supraphysiologic doses of testosterone or other androgens to members 34 

of the female sex. 35

(b) A medical professional who engages in any of the practices identified in subsection 4

(c) This section does not apply to the good-faith medical decision of a parent or guardian 11 

of a minor born with a medically verifiable genetic disorder of sexual development, including 12 

any of the following: 13 

(1) A minor with external biological sex characteristics that are irresolvably 14

virilization, 46 XY chromosomes with undervirilization, or having both 16 

ovarian and testicular tissue. 17 

(2) When a physician has otherwise diagnosed a disorder of sexual development, 18

does not have the normal sex chromosome structure for male or female sexes. 20 

(d) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, it shall be unlawful for any health care 21

SECTION 2.  This act becomes effective October 1, 2023. 25
