AN ACT
To amend chapter 191, RSMo, by adding thereto seven new sections relating to gender

transition procedures, with a delayed effective date.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:

Section A. Chapter 191, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto seven new sections, to
2 be known as sections 191.1720, 191.1725, 191.1730, 191.1735, 191.1740, 191.1742, and
3 191.1745, to read as follows:

191.1720. Sections 191.1720 to 191.1745 shall be known and may be cited as the
2 "Missouri Child and Adolescent Protection (MCAP) Act".

191.1725. For purposes of this section, the following terms mean:
2 (1) "Biological sex", the biological indication of male or female in the context of
3 reproductive potential or capacity, such as sex chromosomes, naturally occurring sex
4 hormones, gonads, and nonambiguous internal and external genitalia present at birth,
5 without regard to an individual's psychological, chosen, or subjective experience of
6 gender;
7 (2) "Cross-sex hormones":
8 (a) Testosterone or other androgens given to biological sex females in amounts
9 that are larger or more potent than would normally occur naturally in healthy biological

10 sex females; and
11 (b) Estrogen given to biological sex males in amounts that are larger or more
12 potent than would normally occur naturally in healthy biological sex males;
13 (3) "Gender", the psychological, behavioral, social, and cultural aspects of being
14 male or female;

15 (4) "Gender reassignment surgery", any medical or surgical service that seeks to
16 surgically alter or remove physical or anatomical characteristics or features that are
17 typical for the individual's biological sex in order to instill or create physiological or
18 anatomical characteristics that resemble a sex different from the individual's biological
19 sex including, but not limited to, genital or nongenital gender reassignment surgery
20 performed for the purpose of assisting an individual with a gender transition;
21 (5) "Gender transition", the process in which an individual transitions from
22 identifying with and living as a gender that corresponds to his or her biological sex to
23 identifying with and living as a gender different from his or her biological sex and may
24 involve social, legal, or physical changes;
25 (6) "Gender transition procedure":
26 (a) Any medical or surgical service, including, but not limited to, physician's
27 services, inpatient and outpatient hospital services, or prescribed drugs, related to
28 gender transition that seeks to:
29 a. Alter or remove physical or anatomical characteristics or features that are
30 typical for the individual's biological sex; or
31 b. Instill or create physiological or anatomical characteristics that resemble a sex
32 different from the individual's biological sex including, but not limited to:
33 (i) Medical services that provide puberty-blocking drugs, cross-sex hormones, or
34 other mechanisms to promote the development of features commonly associated with
35 members of the opposite sex; or
36 (ii) Genital or nongenital gender reassignment surgery performed for the
37 purpose of assisting an individual with a gender transition;
38 (b) The term "gender transition procedure" shall not include:
39 a. Services to individuals born with a medically verifiable disorder of sex
40 development including, but not limited to, an individual with external biological sex
41 characteristics that are irresolvably ambiguous, such as those born with 46,XX
42 chromosomes with virilization, 46,XY chromosomes with undervirilization, or having
43 both ovarian and testicular tissue;
44 b. Services provided when a physician has otherwise diagnosed an individual
45 with a disorder of sexual development and determined through genetic or biochemical
46 testing that the individual does not have normal sex chromosome structure, sex steroid
47 hormone production, or sex steroid hormone action;
48 c. The treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or disorder that has been
49 caused by or exacerbated by the performance of gender transition procedures
50 regardless of whether the gender transition procedure was performed in accordance
51 with state and federal law; or

191.1730. A physician or other health care provider shall not knowingly provide
2 gender transition procedures to any individual under eighteen years of age and shall not
3 refer any individual under eighteen years of age to any health care provider for gender
4 transition procedures.

191.1735. 1. Public funds shall not be directly or indirectly used, granted, paid,
2 or distributed to any individual, entity, or organization that provides gender transition
3 procedures to an individual under eighteen years of age.
4 2. The MO HealthNet program shall not reimburse or provide coverage for
5 gender transition procedures to an individual under eighteen years of age.
6 3. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any speech protected by the
7 First Amendment of the United States Constitution.

191.1740. Any referral for or provision of gender transition procedures to an
2 individual under eighteen years of age shall be considered unprofessional conduct and
3 any health care provider doing so may be subject to discipline by the appropriate
4 licensing entity or disciplinary review board with competent jurisdiction in this state.

191.1742. Sections 191.1720 to 191.1745 shall not apply to the provision of cross-
2 sex hormones to any individual under eighteen years of age who was prescribed cross-
3 sex hormones before January 1, 2024, to treat an inconsistency between the individual's
4 biological sex and the individual's perceived gender or perceived sex.

191.1745. 1. The provision of a gender transition procedure to an individual
2 under eighteen years of age in violation of sections 191.1720 to 191.1745 shall be
3 considered grounds for a cause of action against the physician or health care provider.
4 2. The provisions of chapter 538 shall not apply to any action brought under this
5 section.
6 3. (1) An individual under eighteen years of age may bring an action under this
7 section throughout the individual's minority through a parent or next friend and may
8 bring an action under this section in the individual's own name upon reaching the age of
9 majority at any time from that point until twenty years after reaching the age of

10 majority or four years from the time of discovery by the injured party of both the injury
11 and the causal relationship between the treatment and the injury, whichever date is
12 later.
13 (2) If at the time the individual abused attains eighteen years of age he or she is
14 under other legal disability, the limitation period under subdivision (1) of this subsection
15 shall not begin to run until the removal of the disability.
16 (3) The limitation period in subdivision (1) of this subsection shall not run
17 during a time period when the individual is subject to threats, intimidation,
18 manipulation, fraudulent concealment, or fraud perpetrated by the physician or other

19 health care professional who prescribed or otherwise provided gender transition
20 procedures or by any person acting in the interest of the physician or other health care

22 4. (1) An individual may assert an actual or threatened violation of sections
23 191.1720 to 191.1745 as a claim or defense in a judicial proceeding.

38 5. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, an action under this section may
39 be commenced, and relief may be granted, in a judicial proceeding without regard to
40 whether the individual commencing the action has sought or exhausted available
41 administrative remedies.
42 6. The attorney general may bring an action to enforce compliance with sections
43 191.1720 to 191.1745.
44 7. Sections 191.1720 to 191.1745 do not deny, impair, or otherwise affect any
45 right or authority of the attorney general, the state, or any agency, officer, or employee
46 of the state, acting under any law other than sections 191.1720 to 191.1745, to institute
47 or intervene in any proceeding.

Section B. The enactment of sections 191.1720 to 191.1745 of this act shall become
2 effective on January 1, 2024.
